Layer 2 Scaling Solutions: A Developer's Comparison Guide

Nov 1, 2024 13 min read PumaPulse Engineering Team
← Back to Blog

Ethereum's Layer 2 ecosystem has matured dramatically in 2024. With Optimism, Arbitrum, zkSync Era, and Polygon all offering different security models, performance characteristics, and developer experiences, choosing the right L2 for your DApp is one of the most consequential architectural decisions you'll make — and the wrong choice can mean migrating your entire user base later.

Key Takeaways

  • Optimistic rollups (Arbitrum, Optimism) offer the easiest migration path from Ethereum mainnet — near-perfect EVM equivalence means most contracts deploy without changes.
  • ZK rollups (zkSync Era, StarkNet) provide stronger security guarantees and faster withdrawals, but the proving overhead means higher costs for complex computations.
  • Where your users and liquidity already are matters more than raw performance numbers — deploying on a chain with no ecosystem is building in a ghost town.
  • Multi-chain deployment with LayerZero or Wormhole is increasingly the right answer for protocols targeting broad adoption, despite the added operational complexity.

Optimistic Rollups

Optimistic rollups (Arbitrum and Optimism) work by executing transactions off-chain and posting compressed transaction data to Ethereum mainnet. They're called "optimistic" because they assume all transactions are valid by default — no proof is required for each batch. Instead, there's a 7-day challenge window during which anyone can submit a fraud proof if they detect an invalid state transition. If no valid fraud proof is submitted, the batch is finalized on L1.

The 7-day withdrawal period is the most significant UX limitation of optimistic rollups. When you want to move funds from Arbitrum or Optimism back to Ethereum mainnet, you must wait 7 days for the challenge period to expire. In practice, liquidity bridges (Hop Protocol, Across, Stargate) solve this by providing instant withdrawals in exchange for a small fee — they front the funds immediately and wait out the challenge period themselves. For most users, this makes the 7-day delay invisible.

Arbitrum has emerged as the dominant optimistic rollup by TVL and developer activity. Its Nitro upgrade brought near-perfect EVM equivalence — you can deploy Solidity contracts compiled for Ethereum mainnet on Arbitrum with zero changes in most cases. The Arbitrum ecosystem includes the largest L2 DeFi ecosystem, with Uniswap, Aave, GMX, and hundreds of other protocols deployed. Optimism (now part of the Superchain with Base, Mode, and other OP Stack chains) has a strong ecosystem and the backing of Coinbase's Base chain, which has driven significant retail adoption.

ZK Rollups

ZK rollups use zero-knowledge proofs to cryptographically verify the correctness of every batch of transactions before posting to L1. Unlike optimistic rollups, there's no challenge period — the validity proof is mathematical proof that the state transition is correct. This means withdrawals to L1 can be processed as soon as the proof is verified on-chain, typically within minutes to hours rather than 7 days. The security model is also stronger: you don't need to trust that someone will submit a fraud proof within the challenge window.

zkSync Era (by Matter Labs) is the leading EVM-compatible ZK rollup. It supports Solidity and Vyper contracts with some limitations — certain EVM opcodes behave differently, and some complex contracts require minor modifications. The developer experience has improved dramatically in 2024, and most standard DeFi contracts deploy without issues. zkSync Era's native account abstraction (every account is a smart contract by default) enables powerful UX improvements like gasless transactions and social recovery wallets that aren't possible on standard EVM chains.

StarkNet uses Cairo, a custom language designed for ZK-provable computation, rather than Solidity. This means existing Ethereum contracts cannot be deployed directly — you must rewrite them in Cairo. The tradeoff is that Cairo programs are highly optimized for ZK proving, enabling significantly higher throughput and lower proving costs than EVM-compatible ZK rollups. StarkNet is the right choice for applications that need maximum throughput and are willing to invest in Cairo development — gaming, high-frequency trading, and applications with complex on-chain logic.

Polygon & Sidechains

Polygon PoS is technically a sidechain rather than a true L2 — it has its own validator set and doesn't inherit Ethereum's security directly. This distinction matters for high-value applications: if Polygon's validators collude or are compromised, funds on Polygon PoS could be at risk in ways that aren't possible on true rollups. However, for consumer applications where the primary concern is low fees and fast transactions rather than maximum security, Polygon PoS remains an excellent choice with a massive existing ecosystem and user base.

Polygon zkEVM is a true ZK rollup that inherits Ethereum's security while maintaining EVM compatibility. It's positioned as the "best of both worlds" — Ethereum security with Polygon's ecosystem and brand recognition. The zkEVM has seen growing adoption in 2024, particularly for gaming and NFT applications that need low fees and fast finality. Polygon's AggLayer, which connects multiple ZK chains into a unified liquidity layer, is one of the most ambitious multi-chain infrastructure projects in the ecosystem.

Performance Comparison

Raw TPS numbers are often misleading — what matters for your application is the combination of throughput, latency, cost, and finality time. Arbitrum and Optimism both achieve 40,000+ theoretical TPS but are typically constrained by the Ethereum L1 data availability bottleneck. In practice, both chains handle 10–50 TPS of actual user transactions comfortably, with costs of $0.01–$0.10 per transaction. EIP-4844 (blob transactions, activated in March 2024) reduced L2 data costs by 10–100x, making all rollups significantly cheaper.

zkSync Era currently processes 2,000–5,000 TPS with sub-second soft confirmation and 1–4 hour hard finality (when the ZK proof is verified on L1). Transaction costs are $0.01–$0.05 for simple transfers, slightly higher for complex contract interactions due to proving overhead. StarkNet achieves similar throughput with lower costs for Cairo-native applications. All L2s are orders of magnitude cheaper than Ethereum mainnet, where a simple token transfer costs $2–$20 depending on network congestion.

Code Example — Cross-Chain Message (LayerZero)

contract CrossChainSender is NonblockingLzApp {
    uint16 constant ARBITRUM_CHAIN_ID = 110;

    function sendMessage(string memory message) external payable {
        bytes memory payload = abi.encode(message);
        // Estimate fees before sending
        (uint256 nativeFee,) = lzEndpoint.estimateFees(
            ARBITRUM_CHAIN_ID, address(this), payload, false, ""
        );
        require(msg.value >= nativeFee, "Insufficient fee");
        _lzSend(ARBITRUM_CHAIN_ID, payload, payable(msg.sender),
                address(0), "", msg.value);
    }
}

Our Recommendation

For DeFi protocols: deploy on Arbitrum first. It has the largest TVL, the most active DeFi ecosystem, and the best liquidity for most token pairs. The EVM equivalence means your Ethereum contracts deploy without changes, and the developer tooling (Hardhat, Foundry, The Graph) works identically. Once you've validated product-market fit on Arbitrum, consider expanding to Optimism/Base for additional reach.

For NFT and gaming applications: Polygon PoS or Immutable X (a ZK rollup specifically designed for NFTs and gaming). Both offer near-zero transaction fees, which is critical for applications where users make frequent small transactions. Immutable X provides additional benefits like gas-free minting and trading for NFTs, and has deep integrations with major gaming studios. For gaming applications that need on-chain game logic, StarkNet's Cairo VM is worth the development investment for its throughput advantages.

For privacy-sensitive or high-security applications: zkSync Era or StarkNet. The cryptographic security guarantees of ZK proofs are meaningfully stronger than the economic security of optimistic rollups, and the faster finality is valuable for applications where users need to move funds quickly. For any application where you're unsure, start with Arbitrum — it's the lowest-risk choice with the most mature ecosystem, and you can always expand to other chains later.
